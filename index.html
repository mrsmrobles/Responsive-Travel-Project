Why San Diego?

San Diego, the birthplace of California

Widely recognized as “America’s Finest City,” San Diego is famous for gorgeous weather, miles of white-sand beaches, and some of the nation’s most popular tourist spots. This city is considered the birthplace of California, becoming part of the U.S. during 1848 with the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo, and is now the eighth-largest city in the country. Renowned for its pleasant year-round climate, San Diego draws tourists from around the world due to its exciting variety of popular gardens, parks, museums, and cultural attractions. With gorgeous scenery and endless entertainment options, San Diego is a nationally beloved treasure in Southern California. Whether you want to check out an art exhibit, take a safari tour, learn how to surf, ride roller coasters, or simply relax and enjoy the striking beauty of the coast, there is something fun for everyone in San Diego.

Food

My favorite restaurants in San Diego

Brigantine

Airy waterfront venue specializing in seafood and meat dishes, including oysters, plus brunch and craft drinks.

Service options: Happy hour food · Fireplace · Great cocktails
Located in: Portside Pier

Address:

1360 N Harbor Dr, San Diego, CA 92101

What I like about it

The view alone makes Brigantine worth a visit, absolutely stunning. The location is prime, right by Seaport Village and just steps from the Navy Museum, which makes it a perfect spot for both locals and visitors. Parking was surprisingly easy, which is always a plus in the area.

Seneca Trattoria

Comfortable restaurant offering homestyle pasta & seafood dishes, plus cocktails.

Service options: Outdoor seating · Fireplace · Private dining room
Located in: InterContinental San Diego, an IHG Hotel

Address:

901 Bayfront Ct Level 19, San Diego, CA 92101

What I like about it

Tucked away on the 19th floor of the Intercontinental Hotel, this rooftop bar feels like a secret waiting to be discovered. At first, you might feel a little lost when you step off the elevator—but then you find the hidden door, and suddenly you’re in one of the most unique, lush, and beautifully designed spaces I’ve seen. The interior is filled with greenery, giving it an upscale jungle vibe. It’s sophisticated but relaxed—the kind of place you instantly fall in love with.

Morning Glory

Eatery with wooden seating serving global breakfast, brunch, and boozy coffee.

Service options: Outdoor seating · Great cocktails · Doesn't accept reservations
Located in: Little Italy

Address:

550 W Date St Suite #C, San Diego, CA 92101

What I like about it

Morning Glory is the top brunch spot in Little Italy! The view of the San Diego Bay from the upstairs courtyard is just stunning and the vibe here is unbeatable. You can't go wrong with most of the food and drink choices on their menu. As a local, I really suggest you check it out on any occasion.
